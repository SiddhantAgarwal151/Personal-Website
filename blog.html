Exploring Sweden

Date: September 4, 2024

A Journey Through Stockholm and Beyond
My trip to Sweden was filled with incredible sights, history, and unique experiences. From exploring royal palaces to relaxing with a traditional Fika, here's a look at some of the memorable highlights of my journey.

A Cruise Through Stockholm
I started my adventure with a two-hour cruise around Stockholm. The city's blend of historical architecture and scenic views was mesmerizing. I captured many photos of the beautiful landscapes and the city’s waterfront, making it a perfect introduction to Sweden.

Drottningholm Palace: A Royal Journey
My visit to **Drottningholm Palace** was a highlight. The palace is still home to Swedish royalty, and the preserved **theater house** was particularly fascinating. This historical site gave me insight into Swedish monarchy and its rich past.

Butterfly and Shark Exhibit
The **butterfly and shark exhibit** was a unique experience, offering an up-close view of exotic butterflies and marine life. The mix of animals made it an enjoyable and educational stop.

Fika: A Taste of Swedish Culture
As a vegetarian, my options during **Fika** were somewhat limited, but I enjoyed a delicious treat nonetheless. Fika is a relaxing tradition, and it allowed me to slow down and savor the Swedish way of life for a moment.

Gamla Stan Museum (The Kronor) & Royal Armory
At **Gamla Stan Museum**, I saw treasures from Sweden's past. I also ventured underground to visit the **Royal Armory**, where centuries-old armor and weapons were on display, showcasing Sweden's military history.

The Ice Bar
Visiting the **Ice Bar** was literally a "cool" experience! Everything, from the glasses to the sculptures, was made entirely of ice. I enjoyed a drink in this frosty atmosphere, making it a memorable stop.

Nobel Prize Museum
The **Nobel Prize Museum** offered insight into the history of the prestigious award. I was particularly impressed by the innovative projects and school exhibits on display, which highlighted the creativity and ingenuity of young minds.

Skyview Station: A View of Stockholm
A ride to the top of **Skyview Station** provided a stunning panoramic view of Stockholm. It was an unforgettable experience to see the city from such a high vantage point.

Skansen: History and Nature Combined
I ended my trip with a visit to **Skansen**, an open-air museum and zoo. The **medieval village** with its small farms and houses gave me a glimpse into Sweden’s past. The blend of history, wildlife, and culture made Skansen an enriching experience.

Overall, my journey through Sweden was a mix of history, culture, and natural beauty. Each place I visited added a new layer to my understanding of this incredible country.

Exploring Finland

Date: September 4, 2024

A Cruise to Finland: Fun on the Baltic Sea
My trip to Finland started with a fun-filled cruise from Sweden. The ship had a variety of activities, including ping pong, video games, and casino games, making the journey exciting and enjoyable.

Turku Castle: A Journey Through History
Once I arrived in Finland, my first stop was **Turku Castle**. The castle's rich history and impressive architecture made it a fascinating place to explore, with exhibits that showcased Finland’s past.

Turku Cathedral: A Peaceful Retreat
I also visited **Turku Cathedral**, a beautiful and serene place. The architecture and history of the church were captivating, offering a moment of reflection during my trip.

Overall, my visit to Finland was a perfect blend of fun and history. The cruise provided a lively start, and the sights of Turku left me with lasting memories of this unique part of the world.

Exploring Norway

Date: September 1, 2024

A Journey Through History, Art, and Culture
My recent trip to Norway was an unforgettable blend of history, art, and adventure. From learning about Norway's role in World War II to marveling at the nation's artistic heritage, each experience left a lasting impression. Here are some highlights from my time in Norway.

Roselette: Reflections on Norway’s WWII History
Roselette offered a poignant insight into Norway's complex history during World War II. It was eye-opening to learn about the persecution faced by German-born Norwegians after the war, illustrating how history often repeats itself in unfortunate ways. The paintings depicting people and events from this period were deeply moving, capturing the pain and resilience of those affected.

Vestigial Park: Sculptures and Evolution of Art
Vestigial Park was a unique experience with its collection of sculptures, all created by the artist Vestigial. Although the sculptures were all nude, they were a powerful representation of the human form. The museum next to the park provided an insightful look into how Vestigial's art evolved over time, adding depth to the outdoor experience.

Fram Museum: My Favorite Spot!
The Fram Museum stood out as my favorite place in Norway. This museum went above and beyond with its exhibits, featuring an igloo, an ice chamber with a horror theme, and four floors dedicated to the history of Inuits and Norwegians navigating through water and snow. The best part? Getting to explore a historic ship and imagining what it would have been like to travel on it. Next time, I’d love to check out the second room, which includes an art and movie experience.

Arkushus Fortress: A Glimpse into Norway's Past
The Arkushus Fortress was a treasure trove of history. The castle itself housed intriguing stories about how royalty was kept within its walls. The prison cells were dark and grim, contrasting sharply with the ornate interiors of the castle. The Norway Resistance Museum provided compelling insights into how Norway resisted Hitler's occupation during WWII, highlighting the bravery and resilience of its people. The Norwegian Armed Forces Museum showcased weaponry and armory from various periods, including modern conflicts like Afghanistan, with vessels that were especially impressive. In the outskirts of the fortress, I watched a play performance, even though I couldn't understand the language, and admired the canons strategically positioned by the river.

Munch Museum: Art from the Heart of Norway
The Munch Museum was an art lover’s paradise, spanning 13 floors. I finally got to see The Scream, both the original and other versions, which were carefully preserved and kept away from light to prevent deterioration. I found the other paintings by Edvard Munch to be just as captivating, offering a window into his unique perspective. The 12th floor provided a stunning view of the city, although the 13th floor was unfortunately closed to visitors.

Overall, my time in Norway was a rich blend of cultural and historical exploration. Each place offered a unique perspective on the nation's past and present, making this journey a truly memorable one.

Exploring Denmark

Date: September 1, 2024

A Journey Through Denmark's Rich Culture and Modern Marvels
My visit to Denmark was an incredible journey filled with cultural exploration and modern attractions. From canal tours to historic castles, each experience highlighted the unique charm of this beautiful country. Here are some of the highlights from my time in Denmark.

Copenhagen Canal Tours: Exploring the City by Water
One of the best ways to experience Copenhagen was through a canal tour. It was fascinating to see how many people use boats for their daily commute. The tour offered a unique perspective of the city, highlighting interesting locations and the seamless blend of old and new architecture along the waterfront.

Ragnarok: A Musical Journey
The Ragnarok museum provided a deep dive into the history of music in Danish and American cultures. The interactive exhibits were a highlight, allowing visitors to listen to music, play with sound frequencies, and even create their own band in eight steps throughout the museum. It was a fun and immersive experience, with lots of interesting instruments and band merchandise on display.

Planetarium: A Look into Our Solar System
The Planetarium offered an insightful journey into our solar system and the stars, presented in a captivating format. Interactive elements, like a light display that detected the elevation of sand, made learning about space engaging. Although I was familiar with much of the information, I was surprised to learn about the extensive amount of space debris orbiting the Earth due to defunct satellites, highlighting a growing issue of space pollution.

Rosenborg Castle: A Glimpse into Royalty
Visiting Rosenborg Castle was like stepping back in time. The castle was filled with historical artifacts, including royal jewels and tapestries. The surrounding garden was beautifully maintained, providing a peaceful escape from the city's hustle and bustle. I also had the chance to visit part of the nearby botanical garden, which was a nice addition to the day’s exploration.

Christiansborg Palace: Panoramic Views and Ancient Ruins
At Christiansborg Palace, I enjoyed breathtaking views from the top of the palace, where I could see the city spread out in all directions. The visit to the ancient ruins beneath the palace was equally fascinating, showcasing structures that have stood for thousands of years and telling the rich history of Denmark's capital.

Ripley’s Believe It or Not? and More: Curiosity Unleashed
The Ripley’s Believe It or Not museum was packed with bizarre and intriguing facts from around the world. Highlights included the exhibit on the tallest man on Earth, which was both fascinating and a bit eerie. The museum also featured jump scares and interactive displays, making it a fun experience for all ages.

Cisternerne: An Underground Art Experience
The Cisternerne offered a unique, somewhat eerie experience in an underground chamber filled with spatial and sound art. The dimly lit, echoing halls created a hauntingly beautiful atmosphere that was unlike anything else I experienced in Denmark.

Museum of Illusions: A Mind-Bending Experience
This museum was full of optical illusions and brain-teasing puzzles, perfect for a group visit. While I enjoyed the various exhibits, it would have been more fun with friends to share the experience with. The interactive nature of the illusions made it a great place to challenge your perception of reality.

Home of Carlsberg: A Taste of Denmark's Brewing Heritage
My tour of the Carlsberg brewery provided fascinating insights into the history and process of beer-making. The interactive games helped explain the science behind brewing, and I even got to enjoy a sample of the beer at the end of the tour. Learning about the Carlsberg family’s dedication to perfecting their craft added depth to the experience.

Overall, my trip to Denmark was a delightful mix of cultural immersion, historical discovery, and modern entertainment. Each attraction offered a unique perspective on Danish life and history, making my journey through this beautiful country both enlightening and enjoyable.
